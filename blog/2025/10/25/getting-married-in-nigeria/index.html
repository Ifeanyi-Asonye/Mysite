    Getting Married in Nigeria¶

    Getting married is a beautiful and, at the same time, an overwhelming adventure.

    To keep this short and straightforward, I won't be discussing all the considerations one must take before, during, and after the wedding ceremony. Plenty of resources already cater to this, in fact, my sister's channel covers it in detail, so go check it out here!

    I will, however, offer a warning: these resources are often centered around the preferences, personality, and specific circumstances to mention a few. Be selective, and only pick and choose what works for you from all the advice you encounter. Remember that the wedding is just a ceremony; the marriage is what lasts a lifetime, so plan wisely.

    With that out of the way, this piece focuses on one critical aspect of the preparation process: the legal registry. This process has become increasingly important, as the resulting certificate is typically the only one recognized internationally.

    If you don't feel like navigating all the bureaucracy yourself, you can certainly hire an agent. As the saying goes, “they’ll handle it for you; you just need to appear on the assigned date.” Nevertheless, I still strongly advise that you read through this guide so you are fully informed about the steps and requirements.

    Let's begin!

    What You Need to Know and the Requirements to Provide¶

    The initial application process can often be completed online or manually, but note that the actual marriage ceremony is performed physically, and both parties must be present. (NB: All documents are necessary as they’ll all be uploaded via the online registration portal.)

    Normally, both parties must be at least 18 years old. If either party is under 21 years of age, parental or guardian consent may be required.

    Key Registry Locations and Certificate Types¶

    Before continuing, I'd like to highlight the key Federal Marriage Registries, depending on your city. These are: Ikoyi (Lagos), Abuja (FCT), Benin, Owerri, Port Harcourt, Kano, and Jos.

    While you can also register a marriage and get a certificate from any Local Government Marriage Registry in Nigeria, it is important to note the difference: Federal Marriage Registry certificates are generally more recognized internationally and for immigration purposes due to enhanced security features and compliance with the Ministry of Interior's standards.

    Types of Marriage¶

    There are two primary types of marriage recognized under this process:

    1. Ordinary Marriage
    2. Special Marriage

    The major difference between these two types of marriages lies in the notice period and the associated fee:

    • Ordinary Marriage: Requires a notice period of about 21 days and currently costs approximately ₦79,000 (Nigerian Naira).
    • Special Marriage: Requires a shorter notice period of 7 days and currently costs approximately ₦110,000 (Nigerian Naira).

    Ordinary Marriage¶

    This kind of marriage involves couples that are citizens of Nigeria: The requirements are:

    Requirements for Husband - Ordinary Marriage

    • Birth certificate or declaration of age;
    • Passport photograph;
    • Sworn affidavit of bachelorhood from a law court / Bachelorhood Certificate (If bachelor);
    • Affidavit of marriage (If already married);
    • Proof of National Identification Number (NIN);
    • Evidence of divorce - Decree Nisi and Decree Absolute (If Divorced);
    • Death certificate of previous wife (If a Widower);
    • Evidence of annulment from court (If previous marriage annulled);
    • Sworn affidavits of single with Child/Children (if single with child/children).

    Requirements for Wife - Ordinary Marriage

    • Birth certificate or declaration of age;
    • Passport photograph;
    • Indigene letter/letter of identification from state of origin (Nigerians only);
    • Sworn affidavit of spinsterhood from a law court / spinsterhood certificate (If Spinster);
    • Affidavit of marriage (If already married);
    • Evidence of Divorce - Decree Nisi and Decree Absolute (If divorced);
    • Proof of National Identification Number (NIN);
    • Death certificate of previous husband (If a widow);
    • Evidence of annulment from court (If previous marriage was annulled);
    • Sworn affidavit of single with child/children (if single with child/children);
    • Data page of international passport (if living abroad);
    • Arrival passport stamp page (if living abroad);
    • Residence Permit (if living abroad).

    Special Marriage¶

    This is a case where one of the couple is not a Nigerian citizen. The requirements are:

    Requirements for Husband - Special Marriage

    • Birth certificate or declaration of age;
    • Passport photograph;
    • Indigene letter/letter of identification from state of origin (Nigerians only);
    • Sworn affidavit of bachelorhood from a law court/Bachelorhood Certificate (If bachelor);
    • Affidavit of marriage (If already married);
    • Evidence of divorce - Decree Nisi and Decree Absolute (If divorced);
    • Proof of National Identification Number (NIN);
    • Death certificate of previous wife (If a widower);
    • Evidence of annulment from court (If previous marriage was annulled);
    • Sworn affidavit of single with child/children (if single with child/children);
    • Data Page of International Passport (if living abroad);
    • Arrival Passport Stamp Page (if living abroad);

    Requirements for Wife - Special Marriage

    • Birth certificate or declaration of age;
    • Passport photograph;
    • Indigene letter/letter of identification from state of origin (Nigerians only);
    • Sworn affidavit of spinsterhood from a law court/spinsterhood certificate (If Spinster);
    • Affidavit of marriage (If already married);
    • Evidence of Divorce – Decree Nisi and Decree Absolute (If divorced);
    • Proof of National Identification Number (NIN);
    • Death certificate of previous husband (If a widow);
    • Evidence of annulment from court (If previous marriage was annulled);
    • Sworn affidavit of single with child/children (if single with child/children);
    • Data page of international passport (if living abroad);
    • Arrival passport stamp page (if living abroad);
    • Residence Permit (if living abroad).

    The Online Application and Ceremony Process¶

    Visit https://candb.interior.gov.ng/marriage/ and then sign up.

    Once you select the appropriate marriage type, click on the application from the portal's side panel, read through the instructions, and upload all required documents (it helps to have all documents properly labeled and organized in a particular folder for easy access).

    The last stage of the application is payment. This is generally made easy with the provision of payment gateways; make sure to download or screenshot the payment confirmation page just in case.

    Date Selection, Notice Period, and Local Variations¶

    When filling the form, you will be required to provide an oath-taking date and the marriage date.

    • Ikoyi Registry Dates (Personal Experience Note): Oath-taking (which includes counseling/interview) is typically done on Mondays and Tuesdays, while the marriage ceremony (the joining) takes place usually from Wednesday to Fridays.
    • Notice Period Warning: When selecting dates, make sure to properly estimate the notice period (working days) from when you submitted to the oath-taking date, so your application doesn't get rejected.
    • Local Variation Warning: This schedule and process are specific to the Ikoyi Marriage Registry. Different rules may apply in other locations. Some may require couples to undergo mandatory marriage counseling or attend a seminar and provide proof of attendance as part of the registration process. As an intending couple, you must find out what is applicable in the state where you are applying.
    • Follow-Up: A few days before the oath-taking date, if you have not received an approval notification in your email, visit the registry physically to enquire.

    The Marriage Ceremony¶

    You will be required to come to the registry on the approved date for the ceremony, bearing in mind the following:

    • Attire: Only corporate attire, native attire, wedding dresses, and suits are allowed. No casual wear.
    • Witnesses: You must provide two witnesses, one for each party.
    • Props: Either a Bible (or similar religious text) or two wedding rings.
    • Timing: Come early for the ceremony, and you are welcome to invite friends and family if you choose.

    NB: These is purely from a personal opinion and experience
